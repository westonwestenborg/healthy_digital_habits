üì¢ Reporting Cyberbullying or Online Issues

1

Document the Incident

Take screenshots of harmful messages, posts, or interactions. Save evidence with dates and times.

2

Contact Your Child's School

Report to the principal, counselor, or designated administrator. Most cyberbullying affects school environment.

3

Follow Up

Stay in contact with school officials about the investigation and any support your child needs.

4

Additional Support

If school response is insufficient, contact district office or seek community mental health support.

  • Oregon Health Plan covers many telehealth services

  • BetterHelp/Talkspace for adults (check insurance coverage)
  • Local providers may offer video sessions

Requirements: Reliable internet connection and private space for sessions

üéØ When to Seek Professional Help

Consider professional support if:

  • Family strategies aren't improving the situation after 4-6 weeks
  • Your child shows signs of depression, anxiety, or other mental health concerns
  • Digital wellness issues are affecting school performance significantly
  • There are safety concerns (self-harm, risky online behavior)
  • Family relationships are severely strained
  • You feel overwhelmed and need professional guidance

üí∞ Payment & Insurance

Options for Mental Health Services:

  • Oregon Health Plan (Medicaid): Covers mental health services
  • Private Insurance: Check coverage for mental health benefits
  • Sliding Scale Fees: Many providers offer reduced rates based on income
  • Employee Assistance Programs: May provide free short-term counseling
  • School-Based Services: Often free through district

Tip: Contact providers directly to discuss payment options - many are willing to work with families.

Parent guides for online safety

üìã Emergency Action Plan

A step-by-step guide for handling urgent digital wellness situations

üö® If You Discover Serious Online Threats

  1. Ensure immediate safety - Keep your child with you
  2. Don't delete evidence - Take screenshots of threats
  3. Contact authorities - Call 911 if threats are imminent
  4. Report to school - If it involves other students
  5. Seek professional support - Contact mental health services

üò∞ If Your Child is in Mental Health Crisis

  1. Stay calm - Your child needs you to be stable
  2. Listen without judgment - Let them talk
  3. Remove means of self-harm - Secure dangerous items
  4. Call 988 - Suicide & Crisis Lifeline for guidance
  5. Don't leave them alone - Stay with them or arrange supervision
  6. Seek immediate professional help - Emergency room if needed

üíª If You Discover Inappropriate Online Activity

  1. Stay calm - React thoughtfully, not emotionally
  2. Document what you found - Screenshots with dates/times
  3. Have a private conversation - Discuss with your child calmly
  4. Implement immediate safety measures - Adjust parental controls
  5. Consider professional guidance - Therapist or counselor
  6. Follow up regularly - Check in on their online activities
