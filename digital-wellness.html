Screen Time Guidelines by Age

Evidence-based recommendations from the American Academy of Pediatrics and child development experts

Ages 2-5

Preschoolers
1 hour on weekdays

High-quality, educational content only

Best Practices:

  • Watch together and discuss content
  • Choose slow-paced, educational programs
  • No screens during meals or 1 hour before bed
  • Avoid fast-paced shows with lots of cuts

Ages 6-12

Elementary
Focus on balance

Ensure screens don't replace essential activities

Essential Activities Come First:

  • 9-12 hours of sleep per night
  • At least 1 hour of physical activity
  • Homework and chores completed
  • Family meals and social time

Ages 13-18

Teenagers
Guided independence

Focus on healthy habits and digital citizenship

Key Areas to Address:

  • 8-10 hours of sleep (devices out of bedroom)
  • Regular face-to-face social activities
  • Online safety and digital citizenship
  • Recognition of problematic use signs

Universal Principles for All Ages

Quality over quantity

Choose educational, age-appropriate content over entertainment

Create safe spaces

Bedrooms and dining areas should be screen-free zones

Stay connected

Regular conversations about online experiences and content

Model behavior

Children learn digital habits by watching parents

Practical Strategies That Work

Research-backed techniques you can implement immediately to improve your family's digital wellness

Create Screen-Free Zones

Why it works: Physical boundaries help establish mental boundaries around screen use.

How to implement:

  • Bedrooms: All devices charge outside bedrooms overnight
  • Dining areas: No phones or tablets during meals
  • Car rides: Encourage conversation or listening to music together
  • Study spaces: Remove distracting devices during homework time

Establish "Buffer Times"

Why it works: Gradual transitions prevent meltdowns and help children self-regulate.

How to implement:

  • Morning routine: No screens until after breakfast and getting dressed
  • Before bed: All screens off 1 hour before bedtime
  • Transition warnings: "10 minutes until screen time ends"
  • Activity bridges: Have the next activity ready before ending screen time

Curate Quality Content

Why it works: Educational content can actually support development when used appropriately.

Look for content that:

  • Teaches skills: Problem-solving, creativity, or academic concepts
  • Encourages interaction: Asks questions or prompts participation
  • Has positive role models: Characters who demonstrate respect and kindness
  • Matches attention span: Age-appropriate pacing and length

Co-Engage With Content

Why it works: Parent interaction transforms passive consumption into active learning.

Ways to co-engage:

  • Ask questions: "What do you think will happen next?"
  • Make connections: "That reminds me of when we..."
  • Extend learning: Try related activities offline
  • Discuss values: Talk about characters' choices and consequences
