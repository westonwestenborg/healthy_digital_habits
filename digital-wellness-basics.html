Digital Wellness Basics

Everything parents need to know about healthy screen habits, backed by research from the American Academy of Pediatrics and child development experts.

Start Here: The Fundamentals

Digital wellness isn't about eliminating screensâ€”it's about creating a healthy balance that supports your child's development, family relationships, and overall well-being. These evidence-based guidelines will help you make informed decisions for your family.

Screen Time Guidelines by Age

These recommendations come from the American Academy of Pediatrics and are used by pediatricians nationwide.

Under 18 Months

Infants & Toddlers
Avoid screens except video chatting

Babies learn best through direct interaction with caregivers. Video calling with family members is fine, but other screen time isn't beneficial at this age.

18-24 Months

Early Toddlers
Watch together if you choose to introduce media

If you want to introduce digital media, watch high-quality programming together and help your child understand what they're seeing.

  • Choose educational content (PBS Kids, Sesame Street)
  • Always watch together and interact
  • Keep sessions short (15-20 minutes max)

Ages 2-5

Preschoolers
1 hour of high-quality content on weekdays

This is the age when screens can become educational tools, but quality and interaction are key.

  • Choose programs designed for learning
  • Watch together when possible
  • Talk about what you see
  • Avoid fast-paced programs with lots of distractions
  • No screens during meals or within 1 hour of bedtime

Ages 6+

School Age
Consistent limits that don't interfere with sleep, physical activity, and other essential activities

Rather than strict time limits, focus on ensuring screens don't replace important activities.

  • Homework and chores come first
  • At least 1 hour of physical activity daily
  • 8-12 hours of sleep (depending on age)
  • Regular family time and social interaction
  • Encourage creative and educational screen use

Teens

13-18 Years
Focus on content quality and balance rather than strict time limits

Teens need more autonomy, but they also need guidance about healthy digital habits.

  • Discuss online safety and digital citizenship
  • Help them recognize signs of problematic use
  • Encourage face-to-face social activities
  • Model healthy digital habits yourself
  • Keep devices out of bedrooms at night

Warning Signs to Watch For

These behaviors might indicate it's time to reassess your family's digital wellness approach.

Sleep & Health Issues

  • Difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep
  • Frequent headaches or eye strain
  • Changes in eating habits
  • Complaints of neck or back pain
  • Fatigue during the day

Behavioral Changes

  • Extreme mood changes when screen time ends
  • Increased aggression or defiance
  • Loss of interest in previous hobbies
  • Lying about screen time or online activities
  • Inability to control screen time usage

Social & Academic Impact

  • Avoiding family activities or social events
  • Declining grades or missed assignments
  • Loss of friendships or social skills
  • Preferring online relationships over in-person ones
  • Neglecting responsibilities for screen time

If you notice these signs:

  1. Take our risk assessment to get personalized recommendations
  2. Talk with your child about what you've observed
  3. Gradually adjust limits rather than making sudden changes
  4. Consider professional help if problems persist or worsen

What Makes Content "High-Quality"?

Not all screen time is created equal. Here's how to identify educational, age-appropriate content.

Look For:

  • Educational value - Teaches skills, concepts, or prosocial behavior
  • Age-appropriate pacing - Not too fast or overstimulating
  • Interactive elements - Encourages participation or thinking
  • Positive role models - Characters who solve problems respectfully
  • Clear storylines - Easy to follow and understand
  • Minimal commercial content - Not primarily focused on selling products

Avoid:

  • Violent content - Even cartoon violence can be problematic
  • Fast-paced editing - Quick cuts and flashy effects
  • Inappropriate language - Including mild profanity or disrespectful behavior
  • Scary or intense content - Can cause nightmares or anxiety
  • Heavy advertising - Programs designed primarily to sell toys
  • Adult themes - Romance, complex social issues, or mature content
