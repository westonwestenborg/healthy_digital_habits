DIY Mindful-Moment Break Cards

Create physical reminder cards to place near devices for quick mindful pauses

What You'll Need:

  • üìã 3" x 5" index cards or plain printer paper
  • üñäÔ∏è Pens, markers, or colored pencils
  • ‚úÇÔ∏è Scissors (if cutting paper)
  • üé® Optional: stickers, tape for decoration

Step-by-Step Instructions:

1

Gather Cards

Aim for at least 4-6 cards so one can live near each high-use device

2

Fold into a "Tent"

Make a firm horizontal crease across the center, so it can stand like an upside-down V. Best with cardstock or doubled paper.

3

Label the Front

Write a big, inviting title such as "PAUSE" or "BREATHE"

4

Add 3-Step Prompt

On the back, write a prompt that helps you pause, notice feelings, and choose a mindful action

5

Decorate Together

As a family, color or add stickers so each card feels personal

6

Place & Rotate

Stand cards by devices (TV, computer, game console). Swap prompts weekly to keep them fresh.

Mindful Card Templates

Choose from these proven prompts or create your own:

PAUSE & BREATHE

1. Stop what you're doing

2. Take 3 deep breaths

3. Ask: "How am I feeling right now?"

GRATITUDE PAUSE

1. Look around you

2. Name 3 things you're grateful for

3. Smile and take a deep breath

BODY CHECK

1. Notice how your body feels

2. Stretch your neck and shoulders

3. Blink slowly 5 times

CONNECT

1. Put the device down

2. Look for a family member or pet

3. Give them your full attention for 1 minute

MOVE YOUR BODY

1. Stand up and stretch tall

2. Do 10 jumping jacks or push-ups

3. Notice how your energy feels now

NATURE MOMENT

1. Look out a window

2. Find something in nature (tree, cloud, bird)

3. Watch it for 30 seconds without judgment

üéµ Student Safe Playlist Builder üéß

Build a playlist that keeps your head and heart in a good place. The music we listen to can influence how we feel, how we act, and how we cope.

For Students

Why Music Matters

  • Music can boost your energy, calm your nerves, or help you focus
  • Some lyrics and sounds can also trigger sad or anxious feelings
  • You deserve music that supports your wellbeing

How to Build a Safe Playlist

‚úÖ

Choose songs that make you feel strong, peaceful, or positive

‚úÖ

Pick music that helps you focus, relax, or express yourself

üö´

Avoid songs that leave you feeling angry, overwhelmed, or hopeless

Prompts to Help You Get Started

üòä A song that makes you smile
ü§ó A song that reminds you you're not alone
üòå A song that helps you feel calm
üí™ A song that motivates you to keep going

For Parents & Caregivers

How You Can Help

  • Ask your child about the music they're into without judgment
  • Listen to a few songs together and talk about how they make you feel
  • Encourage playlists that support your child's resilience and wellbeing

Red Flags in Media Content

Be aware of repeated themes like:

  • Hopelessness or self-harm
  • Glorification of violence or substance use
  • Isolation or extreme emotional swings
