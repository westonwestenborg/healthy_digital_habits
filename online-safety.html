Online Safety

Cyberbullying prevention, device controls, and tools to keep your family safe online

Why Online Safety Matters

Our phones, tablets, and computers help us connect, learn, and have fun. But not all online interactions are positive. It's important to recognize harmful behaviors like cyberbullying and know how to stay safe and support one another.

Understanding Cyberbullying

What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is when someone uses technology to harass, threaten, or embarrass others. It can look like:

  • üì± Sending mean or hurtful messages
  • üì¢ Spreading rumors or posting private information online
  • üö´ Excluding others in group chats or online games
  • üë§ Creating fake accounts to impersonate or harass

Impact: Cyberbullying affects wellbeing, school engagement, and feelings of safety, even outside of school.

Warning Signs to Watch For

These changes might indicate your child is experiencing cyberbullying or other online issues:

üì± Digital Behavior Changes

  • Suddenly avoiding devices they used to enjoy
  • Appearing upset after using devices
  • Being secretive about online activities
  • Receiving texts/calls at unusual hours
  • Deleting messages quickly

üòü Emotional & Social Changes

  • Mood changes, especially after device use
  • Withdrawing from family and friends
  • Reluctance to go to school or social events
  • Appearing anxious or sad
  • Loss of interest in activities they used to enjoy

üè´ School & Sleep Changes

  • Declining grades
  • Avoiding school or making excuses
  • Sleep problems or nightmares
  • Changes in eating habits
  • Physical complaints (headaches, stomach aches)

üö® When to Take Immediate Action

Contact school counselors, administrators, or law enforcement if you notice:

  • Threats of violence or self-harm
  • Sharing of inappropriate images
  • Evidence of cyberstalking or harassment
  • Your child expressing thoughts of not wanting to live

Crisis Support: Call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or text HOME to 741741
